After too many stale dates and ghosted chats, Maya almost deleted MatchmakerAI. Instead, she updated her profile with a sarcastic joke: “If you can explain CRISPR without mansplaining, I’ll buy the coffee.” Within 48 hours, she matched with Jonah. His reply? “Only if you explain deep learning without eye-rolling.” What started as banter became a whirlwind of late-night calls and voice notes. MatchmakerAI’s algorithm had paired them based on niche scientific interests, compatible circadian rhythms, and something Maya hadn’t expected: their shared taste in indie jazz and dark humor. Three months later, Jonah flew to Boston “just to meet this glorified RNA enthusiast.” They clicked instantly. By the sixth month, Jonah relocated, citing “better East Coast bagels” as the official reason—but everyone knew the truth.
